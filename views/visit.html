Hours & Fares

Open: 365 Days a Year

Hours: 6:30 a.m. – 12:30 a.m.

Wheelchair Accessible: via the Upper Station at 1220 Grandview Avenue

No Reservations or Advance Tickets Required

Payment Methods: Cash, Port Authority bus passes, and tickets accepted

Standard Fares

  • Adults (12–64): $2.50 each way / $5.00 round trip
  • Children (6–11): $1.25 each way / $2.50 round trip
  • Children 5 and under: Free

Group Rates (10+ people)

  • Adults: $1.75 each way / $3.50 round trip

Field Trip Program for Students & Youth Groups

A free field trip program is available for students and youth groups of ten or more daily – except Saturdays and Sundays after 12:00 noon due to weekend crowds and limited space. The program includes:

  • A teacher's lesson packet for students' specific grade level
  • A round trip ride on the Duquesne Incline
  • A history of inclines in Pittsburgh
  • A discussion about Pittsburgh's industrial past
  • A visit to the machine room to observe the original hoisting equipment in operation and learn about the engineers who built it
  • A panoramic view of Downtown Pittsburgh and The North Shore from our observation deck 400 feet above the three rivers
  • A brief history of the French and Indian War from which the city arose

Frequently Asked Questions

Is parking available?
Yes, free parking is available near the lower station.
Are pets allowed?
Only service animals are allowed aboard the incline.
Can I bring a stroller?
Yes, but you may be asked to fold it during busy times.
Is there a self-guided tour?
Yes! Your visit includes a pictorial history of Pittsburgh’s industry, sports, and inclines, a machine room visit with the original 1877 equipment, and a panoramic view of Downtown Pittsburgh. Be sure to stop by the gift shop for postcards and souvenirs!
Are there other related tours in Pittsburgh?
Yes! Check out Molly's Tolleys or call 412-281-2055
